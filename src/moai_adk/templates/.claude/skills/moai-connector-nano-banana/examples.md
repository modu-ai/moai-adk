## Common Mistakes and Solutions

### Mistake 1: Vague Prompt
❌ **Wrong**: "A nice picture of a mountain"
✅ **Correct**: "A breathtaking mountain landscape at golden hour with snow-capped peaks, dramatic lighting, photorealistic, 4K quality"

### Mistake 2: Missing Technical Details
❌ **Wrong**: "Portrait of a person"
✅ **Correct**: "Professional portrait with 85mm lens, shallow depth of field (f/1.8), soft natural lighting, medium close-up"

### Mistake 3: Conflicting Instructions
❌ **Wrong**: "Photorealistic cartoon style with abstract elements"
✅ **Correct**: "Photorealistic portrait" OR "Cartoon illustration style"

### Mistake 4: No Lighting Specification
❌ **Wrong**: "A product on a table"
✅ **Correct**: "A product on a table with soft diffuse studio lighting from 45-degree angle, creating subtle shadows"

### Mistake 5: Unclear Editing Instruction
❌ **Wrong**: "Make it better"
✅ **Correct**: "Increase brightness by 20%, add warmer color tones, and sharpen the focus on the main subject"

## Advanced Prompt Techniques

### Technique 1: Layered Description

Build prompts in layers for complex scenes:
```
Layer 1 (Subject): A vintage bicycle leaning against a brick wall
Layer 2 (Environment): in a charming European alleyway with cobblestones
Layer 3 (Details): Morning light filtering through overhead vines, creating dappled shadows
Layer 4 (Technical): Photorealistic, 50mm lens, shallow depth of field (f/2.8)
Layer 5 (Style): Warm color grading, cinematic quality, Instagram-worthy
```

### Technique 2: Negative Space Design

Use compositional language:
```
A single red apple centered on a white marble surface with generous negative space.
Minimalist composition, soft top-down lighting, high-key photography style.
```

### Technique 3: Mood-First Approach

Start with emotional tone:
```
A serene and peaceful Japanese tea ceremony scene, evoking calm and mindfulness.
Soft natural light, muted earth tones, intimate close-up shots.
Traditional setting with modern photographic quality.
```

## Best Practices Summary

1. **Be Specific**: Include subject, setting, lighting, camera details
2. **Use Technical Terms**: Lens focal length, aperture, lighting type
3. **Describe Mood**: Emotional tone helps guide generation
4. **Specify Quality**: Resolution, style, photorealistic vs. artistic
5. **Layer Details**: Build from general to specific
6. **Test and Iterate**: Use multi-turn chat for refinement
7. **Choose Right Model**: Pro for quality, Flash for speed
